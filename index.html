There are many reasons why you might want to retain a tax attorney. For some people, having a complicated tax situation—such as foreign assets—is enough reason to work with a law firm specializing in tax issues. In other cases, you may need one if:

  • You owe a large amount of back taxes to the IRS or the state

  • You are being audited by the IRS or by the state of New Jersey

  • You have committed tax evasion (tax fraud)

  • You have unreported foreign bank accounts or assets

  • You have failed to pay state and/or federal payroll taxes on time

  • You are undergoing a New Jersey, New York, or California residency audit or need a state tax relief consult.

While there are many advantages to using an accountant when preparing your taxes, if you have tax issues with the IRS or a state taxing agency, there are significant advantages to being represented by an attorney instead of an accountant. First, your communications with a lawyer are protected by the attorney-client privilege. Although there is a limited federal accountant-client privilege, that privilege goes away if the IRS decides to bring criminal charges against you. Sometimes, your accountant may even be called a witness against you in a tax fraud case.

Second, tax attorneys have extensive training in federal and state tax law. This is critical given the complexity of American tax law. According to the National Taxpayer Advocate, the tax code changes on average one time daily. A tax lawyer can analyze the tax code and the IRS attorneys’ interpretations of the law, court rulings on tax law, and various U.S. Treasury regulations. They can then provide specific legal advice based on your situation and applicable federal and state laws.

If you owe money to the IRS, then you may be able to reduce your tax balance. We can explore several options with you based on your specific situation. This may include submitting an offer in compromise, appealing the amount owed, requesting innocent spouse relief, or even analyzing whether all or part of the taxes owed fall outside the time period they can legally collect.

In some cases, reducing your tax balance may not be possible. If we cannot reduce your tax amount, we can help you decide how to handle the matter best. You may be able to set up an installment agreement or payment plan, consolidate your tax debt, or use another method to tackle your tax debt. Contact us to see what options are available for you.

If you cannot meet the payment deadline imposed by the IRS or state tax authorities, it's crucial to seek advice promptly. Consulting with a tax attorney from Paladini Law is a wise first step. We will help you understand your legal options and develop a strategy to tackle the problem while aiming to protect your financial stability.
